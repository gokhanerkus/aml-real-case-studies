# Analysis — Sanctions Evasion Network

## 1. Overview

This case identifies a cross-border network potentially used for sanctions evasion involving Iranian, Turkish, and UAE-linked entities.

The analysis is based on early-stage open-source intelligence (OSINT) signals.

---

## 2. Key Entities

- Babak Zanjani (sanctioned individual)
- Reza Zarrab (financial intermediary)
- Mahdi Shams (intermediary)
- Onur Air (Turkey-based aviation company)
- Qeshm Air (Iran-linked aviation company)

---

## 3. Key Risk Indicators

### 3.1 Sanctioned Entity Involvement
Babak Zanjani was blacklisted by US and EU authorities for sanctions evasion.

➡️ High sanctions exposure

---

### 3.2 Asset Transfer Pattern
Aircraft linked to a Turkish company were transferred to an Iran-linked airline.

➡️ Indicator:
Use of physical assets to bypass sanctions restrictions

---

### 3.3 Use of Intermediaries
Commercial relationships structured through third parties.

➡️ Indicator:
Obfuscation of beneficial ownership

---

### 3.4 Multi-Jurisdictional Activity
Entities operate across:
- Turkey
- UAE
- Iran
- Additional jurisdictions

➡️ Indicator:
Layering stage in money laundering

---

### 3.5 Trade-Based Mechanisms
Later investigations revealed use of gold trade and fake trade documentation.

➡️ Indicator:
Trade-Based Money Laundering (TBML)

---

## 4. Analytical Insight

This case demonstrates early detection of risk signals before large-scale investigations became public.

The structure shows:
- Network-based laundering
- Sanctions evasion through commercial entities
- Cross-border financial complexity

---

## 5. Risk Assessment

**Risk Level: VERY HIGH**

Drivers:
- Sanctioned individual involvement
- Cross-border structure
- Asset-based transactions
- Intermediary usage

---

## 6. Conclusion

The case presents a high-risk sanctions evasion network with strong indicators of money laundering.

Recommended actions:
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
- Sanctions escalation review
- Suspicious Activity Report (SAR)

---

## 7. Analyst Note

This case highlights the ability to detect early-stage financial crime risks using open-source intelligence.
