# Case Study: Cross-Border Trade Patterns & Potential Illicit Financial Flows

## Context
A review of cross-border trade activity between a high-risk conflict zone and a neighboring country revealed a significant and rapid increase in trade volume within a short timeframe.

The region is known for:
- Ongoing conflict
- Presence of sanctioned / high-risk entities
- Weak regulatory oversight

## Key Observations
- Sharp increase in export volume without corresponding economic fundamentals
- Trade routes overlapping with areas under control of non-state armed groups
- Inconsistent official reporting vs observed activity
- Lack of transparency in counterparties and intermediaries

## Red Flags
- Unusual trade growth in high-risk jurisdiction
- Potential use of intermediaries to obscure origin/destination
- Possible involvement of sanctioned or non-state actors
- Limited verifiable information on source of goods and funds

## Risk Assessment
Risk Level: HIGH

The combination of:
- geographic exposure
- abnormal trade patterns
- lack of transparency

suggests potential use of trade channels for:
- illicit financial flows
- sanctions evasion
- informal / shadow economy activity

## AML Relevance
- KYC / KYB: Counterparty verification challenges
- SoF / SoW: Unclear origin of funds and goods
- Adverse Media: Exposure to conflict-linked activity
- Transaction Monitoring: Pattern inconsistency
- Escalation: Required

## Decision
Recommended Action: ESCALATE

The case requires enhanced due diligence (EDD) and further investigation before any onboarding or transaction approval.

## Notes

"Essentials of Counterterrorism" (Praeger, 2015)
